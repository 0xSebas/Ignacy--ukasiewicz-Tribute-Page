Ignacy Łukasiewicz

The man who brought light into the darkness. Prometheus of the 19th century.

Ignacy Łukasiewicz holding his invention - oil lamp

Most important events in the life of Jan Józef Ignacy Łukasiewicz

  • 1822 - Born in Zaduszniki, Subcarpathia, Poland
  • 1836 - Due to bad financial situation in his family he leaves his school (there he learned to speak latin and german languages). At age of 14 he starts his first job in the pharmacy as a practicant.
  • 1840 - He starts working as Edward Hübl's assistant in his pharmacy in Rzeszów, Poland.
  • 1845 - Ignacy joins Polish Democratic Society a organization fighting for the polish independence during the 123 years of partitions.
  • 1846 - Gets arrested by Austrio-Hungarian authorities in Rzeszów and sent to prison in Lviv (pl: "Lwów"). This prevents the outbreak of the polish independence uprising in Rzeszów, which at that time happend in Cracow (pl: "Kraków").
  • 1847 - Realesed from the prison due to lack of strong evidence. Still he is legaly obliged not to leave the city.
  • 1848 - Thanks to the help of his current employer he can leave the city and begin his studies at the Jagiellonian University. He passes all of his exams except Pharmacognosy.
  • 1852 - Received his master's degree in pharmacy at the University of Vienna
  • 1852/1853 - At the turn of those years he and Jan Zeh recived paraffin oil using fractional distillation.
  • 1853 - Created the first kerosene lamp.
  • 1854 - Started the first in the world oil mine in Bróbka, Subcarpathia, Poland.
  • 1873 - Pope Pius IX grants him the title of Papal gentleman for his charitable activities..
  • 1877 - Was elected an envoy to the "Diet of the Kingdom of Galicia and Lodomeria, and of the Grand Duchy of Cracow" for the 4th term of the said assembly.
  • 1882 - Died died of pneumonia in Chokówka, Subcarpathia, Poland.
